Reasoners

Modern reasoners like HermiT2.9 or Pellet2.10 implement sophisticated approaches and apply heuristics for performance optimization. Most ontology development systems such as ProtÃ©gÃ©2.11 offer users support of a reasoner to identify inconsistent (often called unsatisfiable) class definitions. Such classes cannot have any members and are therefore useless and indicate a fundamental error in the ontology design.

Another function of reasoning systems is the explanation of inferences. Explanation typically involves computing a (hopefully small) subset of the ontology that still entails the inference in question, and if necessary presenting the user with a chain of reasoning steps (). This can help finding mistakes during the development of the ontology.
